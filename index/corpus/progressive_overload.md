# Progressive overload

source: progressive_overload.md

## Definition

Progressive overload means gradually increasing training stress over time so muscles adapt. Stress can come from more weight, more reps, more sets, or shorter rest — not weight alone.

## Practical rules

- Add load only when you can complete all prescribed working sets at the top of your rep range with good form.
- Typical progression: +2.5 kg on upper-body compounds, +5 kg on lower-body compounds, **or** +1–2 reps at the same weight before adding load.
- If performance stalls for 2–3 sessions on the same exercise, hold load and consolidate (same weight, same reps) rather than forcing jumps.

## What counts as progress

- Estimated 1RM trending up over 4–6 weeks is a stronger signal than a single session PR.
- Volume load (sets × reps × weight) should trend up slowly across mesocycles, not spike week to week.

## When not to overload

- After poor sleep, illness, or a sharp drop in performance vs recent trend — maintain or reduce load until recovery normalizes.
