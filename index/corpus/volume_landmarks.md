# Hypertrophy volume landmarks

source: volume_landmarks.md

## Sets per muscle per week

For hypertrophy, weekly **hard sets** per muscle group matter more than a single session's pump.

| Landmark | Sets / muscle / week (approx.) | Notes |
|----------|-------------------------------|-------|
| MEV (minimum effective volume) | ~6–8 | Below this, growth stimulus is often weak for trained lifters |
| MAV (maximum adaptive volume) | ~12–20 | Sweet spot for many intermediates; individual variance is high |
| MRV (maximum recoverable volume) | ~20–25+ | Beyond this, performance and recovery often degrade |

## Session structure

- Most hypertrophy work sits in **6–20 reps** per set for compounds and isolations.
- **2–4 working sets per exercise** is typical; more sets per exercise usually mean fewer exercises per session.
- Stop 0–3 reps shy of failure on most sets unless testing — grinding every set raises fatigue faster than stimulus.

## Application

- Count only **working sets** (exclude warm-ups).
- Spread volume across the week (e.g. PPL) rather than cramming all chest volume into one marathon session.
- If weekly performance trends down while volume is high, you are likely past MRV for that muscle — reduce sets before adding load.
