# Deload and fatigue management

source: deload_fatigue.md

## Signs you need a deload

- Performance below recent trend for multiple sessions despite adequate sleep and nutrition.
- Acute:chronic workload ratio (ACWR) elevated above ~1.3–1.5 for sustained periods.
- Persistent joint soreness, motivation drop, or inability to match prior-week rep counts at the same weight.

## What a deload is

A deload reduces fatigue while keeping movement patterns. It is **not** a full week off unless injury or illness requires it.

## Standard deload prescription

- Reduce working-set volume by **40–60%** (fewer sets or fewer exercises).
- Keep intensity moderate: ~70–80% of recent working weights, or same weight with fewer reps.
- Duration: **1 week**, or until performance delta returns toward baseline.

## ACWR guidance

- ACWR compares recent 7-day volume to a 28-day chronic average.
- Ratio near **0.8–1.2** is often sustainable for many lifters.
- Ratio **> 1.3** suggests acute load is outpacing what you've adapted to — prioritize recovery or deload before adding load.

## After the deload

Resume normal volume gradually (add 1–2 working sets per muscle per week), not all at once.
