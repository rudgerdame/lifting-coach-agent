# Sleep and training recovery

source: recovery_sleep.md

## Why sleep matters

Sleep supports muscle repair, motor learning, and hormonal balance. Observational data in lifters often links short sleep to worse session performance — correlation, not guaranteed causation.

## Practical targets

- **7–9 hours** per night is a reasonable target for most adults who train hard.
- **Consecutive nights under 7 hours** increase cumulative fatigue; the model tracks this via sleep lags and deviation from your trailing baseline.

## Pre-workout signals

- Compare last night's sleep to your **trailing 7-day average** — a large negative deviation is a caution flag for max-effort attempts.
- One bad night is not always reason to skip training; reduce intensity or volume instead of forcing PRs.

## Sleep hygiene (brief)

- Consistent wake time beats perfect weekends-only catch-up.
- Caffeine cutoff ~8 hours before bed if sleep latency is an issue.

## Limitation

This coach uses logged sleep totals only — no sleep stage data. Treat sleep features as one input among load, nutrition, and recent performance.
