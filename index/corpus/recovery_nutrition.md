# Nutrition and training recovery

source: recovery_nutrition.md

## Energy balance

- Chronic large calorie deficits impair recovery and strength progress for many lifters.
- Trailing **7-day calorie and macro averages** are more informative than a single low day.

## Protein

- Hypertrophy-focused lifters often target **1.6–2.2 g protein per kg bodyweight per day**.
- Distribute protein across meals; pre-workout fasted vs fed is secondary to weekly totals.

## Carbohydrates

- Carbs fuel high-volume training. Very low carb intake during heavy blocks can reduce performance on multi-set compounds.
- Track **trailing 7-day carb average** alongside calories when performance dips without obvious load spikes.

## Bodyweight trends

- Slow weight gain (~0.25–0.5% bodyweight per week) can support hypertrophy phases.
- Rapid weight loss while pushing load often correlates with performance regression.

## How this coach uses nutrition

- Features use trailing means and acute-vs-chronic **deviation** — not meal timing or micronutrients.
- Nutrition advice here grounds retrieval citations; personalized numbers come from your Apple Health / recovery CSV aggregates.
